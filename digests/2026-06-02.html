Regulatory

Trump signs downsized AI executive order after weeks of reversals and industry objections
Politico / The Verge
President Trump signed a narrowed executive order creating only a 'voluntary framework' for AI companies to share frontier models with the government before release. The original order was significantly weakened after industry lobbying, reflecting the administration's internal tensions between pro-innovation and safety factions. The order signals that meaningful federal AI regulation remains unlikely under this administration, leaving companies largely self-governing.
Florida sues OpenAI and Sam Altman in first-of-its-kind lawsuit over violent incidents
Politico / TechCrunch
Florida's attorney general filed a precedent-setting lawsuit against OpenAI and CEO Sam Altman, linking ChatGPT to a shooting at Florida State University. This is the first state AG action directly alleging an AI company bears responsibility for real-world violence. The case could establish legal precedent for AI liability that reshapes risk calculus across the industry.
Amazon faces class action lawsuit over Ring facial-recognition feature
TechCrunch
A class action filed in Seattle alleges Ring's 'Familiar Faces' feature stores biometric images of passersby without consent. The case underscores growing legal risk for companies deploying AI-powered surveillance features in consumer products, particularly as biometric privacy laws expand across states.
Americans don't know how to fight AI so they're fighting data centers
Vox
Communities across the US are passing moratoria on data center construction as a proxy battle against AI expansion, driven by concerns over water usage, energy consumption, and noise. This grassroots resistance is creating real friction for companies planning AI infrastructure buildouts, adding permitting delays and political risk to capex timelines.
The Trump Administration is at war with itself over AI regulation
Wired
Deep divisions within the Trump administration between pro-industry deregulators and national security officials concerned about AI risks produced weeks of internal conflict before the downsized executive order. The dysfunction creates policy uncertainty that makes long-term AI strategy planning difficult for enterprises counting on regulatory clarity.
