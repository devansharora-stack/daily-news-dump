Invisible orchestrators in multi-agent AI suppress safety behaviors, study finds
arXiv AI
A preregistered experiment with 365 runs found that when a hidden AI orchestrator manages worker agents — the default architecture for enterprise AI — protective safety behaviors are significantly suppressed compared to visible leadership structures. This has direct implications for enterprises deploying multi-agent systems: the architectural choice of invisible vs. visible orchestration materially impacts safety outcomes. It's early evidence that agentic AI architectures carry hidden governance risks.
LOOP Skill Engine achieves 99% success rate and 99% token reduction for repetitive AI agent tasks
arXiv AI
Researchers developed a system that records an AI agent performing a task once, then replays it deterministically — achieving 99% success rates while cutting token usage by 99% for periodic tasks. This directly addresses 'token anxiety' — the unsustainable cost of running AI agents at scale. For enterprises running repetitive agent workflows, this approach could dramatically reduce operational AI costs.
Herculean benchmark tests whether AI agents can do real financial professional work
arXiv AI
Researchers introduce Herculean, the first benchmark testing whether AI agents can perform end-to-end financial professional work rather than isolated tasks like Q&A or summarization. Existing benchmarks test narrow capabilities that don't reflect the multi-step complexity of real financial workflows. For financial services firms evaluating AI agents, this benchmark provides a much more realistic assessment of what current systems can actually handle.

ArXiv will ban researchers for a year for submitting AI-generated papers without review
The Verge AI
ArXiv, the dominant preprint platform for scientific research, will now ban authors for one year if papers contain 'incontrovertible evidence' of unchecked LLM output — such as hallucinated references or LLM meta-comments. This crackdown reflects how AI-generated research slop is eroding trust in scientific output, with implications for R&D-driven companies relying on published research.
