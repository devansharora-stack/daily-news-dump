AI Workforce Impact

TechCrunch / Hacker News
General Motors laid off hundreds of IT employees and plans to backfill positions with workers who have stronger AI capabilities. This signals that major non-tech enterprises are now actively restructuring headcount around AI competency, not just adding AI roles on top of existing teams.

Ars Technica / Hacker News
Amazon employees report inflating their AI tool usage metrics — dubbed 'tokenmaxxing' — to satisfy internal pressure to demonstrate AI adoption. This is a textbook example of the GenAI Paradox: broad adoption metrics that mask the absence of genuine productivity gains, and a warning sign for any company using usage volume as a proxy for AI value.
